## üìö All 14 Canon Types Explained (For Everyone)

### üü¢ **Beginner-Friendly Canons** (Start Here!)

#### 1. **Strict Canon** - "The Classic Round"
**What it does**: Creates a simple round where one voice follows another at a fixed distance.

**Best for**: Learning canons, traditional rounds like "Row, Row, Row Your Boat"

**How to use**:
- Canon Type: **Strict Canon**
- Interval: **12** (octave - safe and harmonious)
- Delay: **4** beats (gives voices breathing room)
- Voices: **2** (keep it simple)

**Try this**: Make a 8-note theme and use these settings. It will sound like Pachelbel's Canon!

---

#### 2. **Perpetual Canon** - "Endless Round"
**What it does**: Creates a canon that can loop forever without awkward transitions.

**Best for**: Rounds, meditation music, endless loops

**How to use**:
- Canon Type: **Perpetual Canon**
- Interval: **12** (octave)
- Delay: **4** beats
- Voices: **2-3**

**Try this**: Create a 12-note theme that ends on the same note it starts. Perfect for looping!

---

### üü° **Intermediate Canons** (After Mastering Basics)

#### 3. **Canon at the Fifth** - "Harmonic Richness"
**What it does**: Creates a canon where the follower is a perfect fifth higher.

**Best for**: Rich harmonies, church music, traditional polyphony

**How to use**:
- Canon Type: **Canon at the Fifth**
- Delay: **4** beats
- Voices: **2-3**

**Try this**: The fifth (7 semitones) creates beautiful harmonies. Use with a simple major scale theme.

---

#### 4. **Canon by Inversion** - "Upside Down"
**What it does**: The follower plays the melody upside-down (intervals reversed).

**Best for**: Contrary motion, Bach-style counterpoint, mirror effects

**How to use**:
- Canon Type: **Canon by Inversion**
- Interval: **12** (octave)
- Delay: **4** beats
- Inversion Axis: **60** (Middle C)

**Try this**: Create a simple ascending scale (C-D-E-F-G). The follower will descend!

---

#### 5. **Rhythmic Canon** - "Tempo Change"
**What it does**: The follower plays the same melody but at a different speed.

**Best for**: Creating texture, augmentation/diminution effects

**How to use**:
- Canon Type: **Rhythmic Canon**
- Interval: **12** (octave)
- Delay: **4** beats
- Mensuration Ratio: **2.0** (follower 2x slower)

**Try this**: Fast theme in the leader, slow and majestic in the follower!

---

#### 6. **Per Augmentationem** - "Majestic Slowdown"
**What it does**: Specialized rhythmic canon where follower is exactly 2x slower.

**Best for**: Creating gravitas, baroque-style augmentation

**How to use**:
- Canon Type: **Per Augmentationem**
- Interval: **12** (octave)
- Delay: **4** beats

**Try this**: Quick, energetic theme becomes slow and stately in the follower.

---

#### 7. **Per Tonos (Modulating)** - "Key Changes" ‚ú® NEW!
**What it does**: Each voice enters at progressively higher keys, creating a sequence.

**Best for**: Sequences, modulating passages, dramatic builds

**How to use**:
- Canon Type: **Per Tonos (Modulating)**
- Interval: **4** (major third)
- Delay: **4** beats
- Voices: **3**

**Try this**: Creates an ascending sequence - Voice 1 in C, Voice 2 in E, Voice 3 in G#. Very dramatic!

---

### üî¥ **Advanced Canons** (For Experienced Users)

#### 8. **Crab Canon** - "Backward"
**What it does**: The follower plays the melody backward in time (retrograde).

**Best for**: Palindromes, Bach-style puzzles, symmetrical structures

**How to use**:
- Canon Type: **Crab Canon**
- Delay: **4** beats

**Try this**: Create a theme like C-D-E-F-G-F-E-D-C. When played backward, it creates interesting patterns!

---

#### 9. **Retrograde-Inversion** - "Double Mirror"
**What it does**: Combines backward (retrograde) AND upside-down (inversion).

**Best for**: Maximum transformation, advanced counterpoint, modernist music

**How to use**:
- Canon Type: **Retrograde-Inversion**
- Interval: **12** (octave)
- Delay: **4** beats
- Inversion Axis: **60** (Middle C)

**Try this**: The follower is both inverted AND reversed - the ultimate transformation!

---

#### 10. **Double Canon** - "Two Canons at Once"
**What it does**: Creates TWO independent canons playing simultaneously.

**Best for**: Complex textures, late baroque style, 4+ voice polyphony

**How to use**:
- Canon Type: **Double Canon**
- Interval: **12** (octave)
- Delay: **2** beats (shorter for clarity)

**Try this**: Automatically creates 4 voices - two separate canonic structures interweaving.

---

#### 11. **Mensuration Canon** - "Multiple Speeds"
**What it does**: Multiple voices at different tempo ratios (like 3:2 or 4:3).

**Best for**: Renaissance polyphony, Ockeghem-style complexity

**How to use**:
- Canon Type: **Mensuration Canon**
- Interval: **12** (octave)
- Delay: **4** beats
- Mensuration Ratio: **1.5** (3:2 ratio)

**Try this**: Creates polytemporal effects - voices moving at different speeds but staying together!

---

#### 12. **Per Arsin et Thesin** - "Upbeat/Downbeat" ‚ú® NEW!
**What it does**: The follower enters on the upbeat (off-beat), creating syncopation.

**Best for**: Syncopation, Renaissance vocal style, metric displacement

**How to use**:
- Canon Type: **Per Arsin et Thesin**
- Interval: **12** (octave)
- Delay: **3.5** beats (automatically adds half-beat)

**Try this**: The follower enters on the "and" of beat 4, creating a syncopated effect!

---

#### 13. **Enigma Canon** - "Hidden Puzzle" ‚ú® NEW!
**What it does**: Combines multiple transformations - selective inversion + speed change.

**Best for**: Puzzle canons, educational examples, complex transformations

**How to use**:
- Canon Type: **Enigma Canon**
- Interval: **5** (perfect fourth)
- Delay: **4** beats
- Inversion Axis: **60** (Middle C)

**Try this**: The follower is transformed in mysterious ways - every other note inverted plus 1.5x speed!

---

#### 14. **Per Motum Contrarium** - "Contrary Motion" ‚ú® NEW!
**What it does**: Traditional Latin name for inversion canon, specifically at unison.

**Best for**: Mirror writing, Fux-style counterpoint, educational examples

**How to use**:
- Canon Type: **Per Motum Contrarium**
- Interval: **0** (unison - same starting pitch)
- Delay: **4** beats
- Inversion Axis: **60** (Middle C)

**Try this**: Leader goes up, follower goes down at the same pitch - perfect mirror!

## üí° Pro Tips

### For Beginners
1. **Start simple**: Use Strict Canon with octave interval
2. **Keep themes short**: 8-12 notes maximum
3. **Use 2 voices**: Add more as you get comfortable
4. **Standard delay**: 4 beats is almost always good
5. **Test instruments**: Piano + Violin is a safe combo

### For Intermediate
1. **Try fifth intervals**: More interesting than octaves
2. **Experiment with delay**: 2-6 beats for different effects
3. **Use 3-4 voices**: Richer without being overwhelming
4. **Mix instruments**: Try contrasting timbres (Piano + Flute)
5. **Add to Song Suite**: Build complete compositions

### For Advanced
1. **Combine transformations**: Use multiple canon types in one piece
2. **Complex rhythms**: Try longer themes with varied rhythms
3. **Modal exploration**: Use different modes for each canon
4. **MIDI export**: Analyze in DAW for deeper understanding
5. **Historical study**: Compare with actual baroque examples

## üéØ Common Questions

### Q: Why do some canons sound "off"?
**A:** Try these fixes:
- Reduce interval (12 or 7 usually works)
- Increase delay (4-6 beats)
- Simplify theme (fewer notes, smoother motion)
- Check mode compatibility

### Q: Which canon type should I start with?
**A:** Start with **Strict Canon** at octave (12 semitones). It's the most forgiving.

### Q: How many voices should I use?
**A:** 
- Beginners: 2 voices
- Intermediate: 3-4 voices
- Advanced: 4-6 voices

### Q: What's the difference between aliases?
**A:**
- **Per Augmentationem** = Rhythmic Canon with 2.0 ratio
- **Ad Diapente** = Strict Canon at fifth (7 semitones)
- **Mensurabilis** = Rhythmic Canon with ratios
- **Perpetuus** = Crab Canon designed to loop
- **Per Motum Contrarium** = Inversion Canon at unison

### Q: Can I use canons in the Song Creation Suite?
**A:** YES! After generating a canon:
1. Go to "Complete Song Creation Suite"
2. Switch to "Compose" tab
3. Your canon appears in "Available Components"
4. Drag it onto the timeline
5. Arrange with other compositions
